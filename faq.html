Tax Tips & FAQ

When should I lodge my tax return?

Usually by October 31 unless using a registered agent..

What expenses can I claim?

Work-related expenses, tools, and certain travel costs.

Do I need a bookkeeper?

If you run a business, bookkeeping helps maintain accuracy.

Maximise Your Work-Related Deductions

Claim expenses directly related to earning your income (but not reimbursed). Common claims such as Work-related travel (not commuting), Tools, uniforms, or protective gear, Home office expenses. And make sure to keep receipts or records—the Australian Taxation Office requires proof.

Claim Working From Home Properly

You can use:Fixed rate method (simpler), Actual cost method (more detailed, often higher claim) and track hours worked from home—this is essential.

Don’t Forget Small Expenses

Phone and internet (work-use portion), Subscriptions (software, professional memberships) and these small things add up over the year.

Be Smart With Car Claims

Choose the best method: Cents per km (simple) / Logbook method (often better for heavy use) / Keep a logbook for 12 weeks to maximise claims.

Lodge On Time (or Use an Accountant)

Lodging yourself: usually due by 31 October, Using a registered tax agent can give you more time

Avoid Red Flags

Overclaiming without evidence, Claiming personal expenses as work-related, Guessing instead of keeping records
